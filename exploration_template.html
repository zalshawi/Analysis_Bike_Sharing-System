Introduction¶

Bay Wheels is a regional public bicycle sharing system in California's San Francisco Bay Area. It was established as Bay Area Bike Share in August 2013. After Motivate's acquisition by Lyft, the system was renamed to Bay Wheels in June 2019. The system is expected to expand to 7,000 bicycles around 540 stations in San Francisco, Oakland, Berkeley, Emeryville, and San Jose.

Bay Wheels, consists of a fleet of specially designed, sturdy and durable bikes that are locked into a network of docking stations throughout the city. It's a fun, convenient and affordable way to get around.

Data Definition¶

The following data definition was inspired from metro which is other bike share system

  • ride_id: Locally unique integer that identifies the ride
  • rideable_type: The kind of bike used on the trip, including electric bikes, docked bikes.
  • started_at: The date/time when the trip began, presented in ISO 8601 format in local time
  • ended_at: The date/time when the trip ended, presented in ISO 8601 format in local time
  • start_station_name: The station name where the trip originated
  • start_station_id: The station ID where the trip originated
  • end_station_name: The station name where the trip terminated
  • end_station_id: The station ID where the trip terminated
  • start_lat: The latitude of the station where the trip originated
  • start_lon: The longitude of the station where the trip originated
  • end_station: The station ID where the trip terminated
  • end_lat: The latitude of the station where the trip terminated
  • end_lon: The longitude of the station where the trip terminated
  • member_casual: The status of the bike rider
